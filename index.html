I am a computer science graduate student at The George Washington University. My area of concentration is Machine Intelligence and Cognition. A keen researcher with a number of inventions in the patent pipeline, I am the founder of my own AI innovation lab - Meraki. My aim is to build a lifetime career in the field of applied artificial intelligence and I am on an active lookout for industrial internship opportunities in the same area.

My Projects

Ongoing

HARP proposes the delivery of CBT to the subject via the medium of a simple pac-man game based on a reinforcement learning based Dynamic Difficulty Adjustment system. The game will seek to maximize user engagement and thus alter the thought pattern, thereby facilitating the reduction of anxiety levels. In the end, we hope to arrive at a cheaper but quality alternative to existing expensive EEG hardware, an open-source GUI for real-time EEG visualization, and an RL-driven game.
(learn more)

Ongoing

Placid is a smart scheduler that, instead of making lists, makes custom schedule recommendations. The ML engine of the web app creates daily goals to optimize performance. The app is designed to maximize user satisfaction and increase productivity in the long run.
(learn more)

January 2021

This research study tests the applicability of popular WSN protocols in the agriculture field with the end goal of optimizing the land output and minimizing water intake. The use of a hybrid clustering-based protocol is eventually done to create a smart agriculture system capable of reducing irrigation water consumption by 38%. The research has currently been submitted to a journal and is under review.

The capstone project I created as a part of my summer training project. It is a recommendation system driven by clustering algorithms and foursquare API that recommends a neighborhood similar in characteristics to the one a user currently resides in.
